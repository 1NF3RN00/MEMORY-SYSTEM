# Graph Pollution Specification

## Overview

The middleware monitors:
semantic graph pollution.

Graph pollution includes:

* weak relationships
* noisy semantic links
* stale adjacency
* excessive traversal density
* low-value graph expansion

Graph pollution reduces:
retrieval precision.

---

# Core Philosophy

Large graphs naturally accumulate:
low-value relationships over time.

The middleware prioritizes:
high-quality semantic connectivity.

---

# Pollution Signals

Examples:

* low traversal success
* weak reinforcement
* failed reranking influence
* low semantic coherence
* excessive expansion noise

Pollution signals remain:
observable and measurable.

---

# Pollution Recovery

Recovery systems may trigger:

* relationship pruning
* traversal threshold increases
* graph reweighting
* adjacency reevaluation

Recovery remains:
deterministic and governed.

---

# Pollution Philosophy

The middleware values:
high-quality semantic graphs,
not maximum graph size.
